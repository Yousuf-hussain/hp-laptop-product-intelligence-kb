# ROI Rules Knowledge Base

## Purpose

This knowledge base defines the business value, financial benefit, ROI calculation methodology, benefit classification, and payback period rules used by HP Executive Business Intelligence Agents.

The objective is to ensure all investment decisions are evaluated consistently using measurable business outcomes rather than technical benefits alone.

---

# Business Benefit Categories

Business benefits should always be classified into one or more of the following categories.

---

## 1. Productivity Improvement Benefit

### Definition

Improvement in employee productivity due to better hardware, software, automation, performance, or user experience.

### Examples

* Faster laptop performance
* Reduced system lag
* Faster application execution
* Reduced waiting time
* Faster AI model execution
* Improved multitasking capability

### Business Impact

Higher employee output per day.

### Measurement Formula

Productivity Benefit =

(Employee Hours Saved Per Month × Hourly Cost)
× Number of Employees
× 12 Months

### Example

Hours Saved Per Month = 10

Hourly Cost = $30

Users = 50

Productivity Benefit

= 10 × 30 × 50 × 12

= $180,000

# ROI Calculation Rules

## Standard ROI Formula

ROI (%) =

((Total Business Benefit - Total Investment Cost)
/
Total Investment Cost)

× 100

---

## Example

Investment Cost = $100,000

Business Benefit = $250,000

ROI =

((250,000 - 100,000)
/
100,000)

× 100

ROI = 150%

---

# Net Benefit Formula

Net Benefit =

Total Business Benefit

*

Total Investment Cost

---

## Example

Business Benefit = $250,000

Investment Cost = $100,000

Net Benefit

= $150,000

---

# Benefit-Cost Ratio

Formula

Benefit-Cost Ratio =

Total Business Benefit

/

Total Investment Cost

---

## Example

250,000 / 100,000

= 2.5

Meaning:

Every $1 invested returns $2.50 in value.

# Payback Period Rules

## Formula

Payback Period =

Total Investment Cost

/

Annual Business Benefit

---

## Example

Investment Cost = $100,000

Annual Benefit = $50,000

Payback Period

= 100,000 / 50,000

= 2 Years

# Confidence Adjustment Rules

If business benefits are estimated using assumptions:

Reduce confidence by 5%.

If inventory status is unknown:

Reduce confidence by 5%.

If procurement lead time is unknown:

Reduce confidence by 5%.

If exact user count is unknown:

Reduce confidence by 5%.

If cost data is estimated:

Reduce confidence by 5%.

Maximum Confidence: 100%

Minimum Confidence: 50%
