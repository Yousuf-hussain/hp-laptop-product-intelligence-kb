# HP Executive Decision & Leadership Recommendation Framework

## Purpose

This framework provides the final executive decision-making logic for evaluating business, technology, inventory, procurement, infrastructure, AI, product, and employee-related requirements.

The framework consolidates:

- Business Value Assessment
- ROI Assessment
- Strategic Alignment
- Business Priority
- Opportunity Cost
- Business Risks
- Customer Success Impact
- Competitive Position

The objective is to determine:

1. Should HP approve the requirement?
2. Should HP review the requirement?
3. Should HP defer the requirement?
4. Should HP reject the requirement?
5. What actions should leadership take?

---

# SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE DECISION FACTORS

The Business Intelligence Agent must evaluate all of the following before making a recommendation.

## Factor 1: Business Value

Question:

How much value does the requirement create?

Assessment Sources:

- Productivity Improvement
- Operational Efficiency
- Innovation Enablement
- Customer Success Impact
- Employee Experience
- Cost Optimization

Classification:

| Business Value Score | Classification |
|---------------------|---------------|
| 90-100 | Exceptional |
| 75-89 | High |
| 50-74 | Moderate |
| 30-49 | Low |
| Below 30 | Minimal |

---

## Factor 2: ROI Assessment

Question:

Is the investment financially justified?

Assessment Criteria:

|--------|---------------|

| 1-49% | Low ROI |

# SECTION 2: DECISION MATRIX

## APPROVE

Conditions:

Business Value:
High or Exceptional

ROI:
Positive

Strategic Alignment:
Strong

Business Priority:
High or Critical

Opportunity Cost:
Moderate or High

Customer Success:
Positive

Competitive Position:
Strong

Business Risks:
Acceptable

Meaning:

The requirement creates significant business value and should proceed.

Leadership Action:

APPROVE

---

## APPROVE WITH REVIEW

Conditions:

Business Value:
Moderate

ROI:
Positive but uncertain

Inventory:
Validation required

Procurement:
Validation required

Strategic Alignment:
Moderate

Risks:
Manageable

Meaning:

The requirement is beneficial but requires additional validation.

Leadership Action:

APPROVE WITH REVIEW

---

## APPROVE WITH PROCUREMENT VALIDATION

Conditions:

Business Value:
High

ROI:
Positive

Inventory:
Unavailable or limited

Procurement:
Required

Supplier Dependency:
Present

Meaning:

Business value is strong, but procurement confirmation is required.

Leadership Action:

APPROVE WITH PROCUREMENT VALIDATION

---

## HOLD

Conditions:

Business Value:
Unclear

ROI:
Uncertain

Demand:
Weak

Priority:
Low

Benefits:
Not fully validated

Meaning:

## REJECT

Low

ROI:
Negative

Strategic Alignment:
Weak

Customer Success Impact:
Low

Competitive Benefit:
Minimal

Risks:
Exceed expected benefits

REJECT
