# HP Strategic Alignment & Business Priority Framework

## Purpose

This framework helps determine:

1. Whether a requirement aligns with HP's strategic objectives.
2. The business importance of the request.
3. The urgency of the request.
4. The expected organizational impact.
5. Whether leadership should prioritize the investment.

The framework is used by:

- Leadership Teams
- Product Teams
- Customer Success Teams
- Finance Teams
- Procurement Teams
- Operations Teams

# SECTION 4: EXECUTIVE INTERPRETATION

## Critical Priority

Characteristics:

- Strong strategic alignment
- High business impact
- High customer impact
- High urgency

Leadership Action:

APPROVE IMMEDIATELY

---

## High Priority

Characteristics:

- Clear business value
- Strong strategic relevance
- Significant operational benefits

Leadership Action:

APPROVE

---

## Medium Priority

Characteristics:

- Benefits exist
- Moderate business value
- Additional validation may be needed

Leadership Action:

APPROVE WITH REVIEW

---

## Low Priority

Characteristics:

- Limited business impact
- Weak strategic alignment

Leadership Action:

HOLD

---

## Minimal Priority

Characteristics:

- Minimal value
- Weak justification

Leadership Action:

REJECT

# SECTION 5: LEADERSHIP QUESTIONS

The Business Intelligence Agent must answer:

1. Does this request support HP's strategic goals?
2. Does it support AI transformation?
3. Does it support digital modernization?
4. Does it improve employee productivity?
5. Does it improve customer success?
6. Does it improve operational excellence?
7. How urgent is the request?
8. What is the business priority score?
9. What is the priority classification?
10. What action should leadership take?
