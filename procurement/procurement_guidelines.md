# Procurement Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines procurement assessment rules used for HP laptop requirement analysis.

The objective is to determine whether requested laptop configurations, components, or customizations require procurement activities and to assess associated sourcing risks, lead times, approvals, and vendor dependencies.

---

# Procurement Objectives

The procurement team is responsible for:

* Sourcing required hardware
* Managing supplier relationships
* Ensuring component availability
* Controlling procurement costs
* Minimizing supply chain risks
* Supporting customer delivery timelines

---

# Procurement Classifications

## No Procurement Required

Definition:

Requested laptop configuration already exists in inventory.

Examples:

* HP EliteBook i7 / 16GB / 512GB SSD
* HP ProBook i5 / 16GB RAM

Action:

Allocate Existing Inventory

Risk Level:

Low

---

## Standard Procurement Required

Definition:

Requested configuration is part of the approved HP product portfolio but not currently available in inventory.

Examples:

* HP ZBook with 32GB RAM
* HP Spectre with 1TB SSD

Action:

Place Standard Procurement Order

Risk Level:

Medium

---

## Custom Procurement Required

Definition:

Customer requests a specialized configuration requiring non-standard components.

Examples:

* 128GB RAM Workstation
* High-End Professional GPU
* Specialized Security Hardware

Action:

Custom Procurement Process

Risk Level:

High

---

## Strategic Procurement Required

Definition:

Requirement involves new technology, new vendor onboarding, or custom product development.

Examples:

* New AI Accelerator
* Custom Motherboard
* Emerging Hardware Platforms

Action:

Executive Procurement Review

Risk Level:

Very High

# Agent Decision Rules

If Requested Configuration Exists In Inventory:

Classification:

No Procurement Required

Recommendation:

Allocate Existing Device

---

If Product Exists But Inventory Unavailable:

Classification:

Standard Procurement Required

Recommendation:

Place Procurement Order

---

If Specialized Hardware Requested:

Classification:

Custom Procurement Required

Recommendation:

Initiate Vendor Sourcing

---

If Requirement Needs New Hardware Platform:

Classification:

Strategic Procurement Required

Recommendation:

Escalate For Executive Review

---

# Procurement Recommendation Priority

Agent must follow this order:

1. Existing Inventory Allocation
2. Inventory Upgrade
3. Inventory Transfer
4. Standard Procurement
5. Custom Procurement
6. New Product Development

---

# Business Recommendation

Procurement should always be treated as the final option after inventory assessment.

The preferred fulfillment strategy is:

Existing Inventory → Upgrade → Transfer → Procurement → Development

This approach minimizes cost, reduces lead times, lowers supply chain risk, and improves customer satisfaction.
